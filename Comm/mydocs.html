INTRODUCTION TO COMMUNICATION SKILLS

Communication- is the process of passing information from one person to another using a given channel of communication

Purposes of communication

Includes;-

  1. Employees are able to follow their job descriptions and can communicate their grievances to their managers
  2. It ensures that companies maximize profits through effectivecommunication with the customers
  3. It allows business to achieve its corporate objective because of good human relations and motivation among the employees
  4. Through effective communication, a business/a company can easily build its image or enhance its reputation

    5. Principles of effective communication

    They are also known as the C's of communication and they include;-

      Clarity- This means that the communication must be clear which is enhanced through the following ways;-

      • By using appropriate means/channel of communication to pass information to the audience
      • By avoiding the use of jargon language.i.e Avoiding the use of sheng' and slang language
      • By proper pronunciation of words to enhance clarity of information
      • By explaining terms that are not familiar with the audience so that an information may be understood

        • Conciseness- this means being brief and straight to the point.It can be enhanced in the following ways;-

      • Focusing on the key points while avoiding excessive details
      • By giving an information that concerns the audience
      • By using bullet points to present the information clearly and concisely

        • Coutesy- this is the use of polite language and words.i.e. Sorry,pardon .e.t.c It ca be enhanced through;-

      • Avoid the use of jargon language(Do not use sheng'/slang language)
      • By being mindful of your tone
      • By speaking politely to others
      • By use of polite words like, excuse me, sorry, please
      • By apologising for any mistakes committed
      • By providing information that is required
      • By doing the right thing at the right time and at the right place

        • Correctness-This principle holds that;-

      • Spelling or grammatical error should be avoided
      • If one is not sure of a particular word, he/she should check the meaning in the dictionary
      • Get clear information from an office if you are not sure of something
      • Proof read your work before submission

        • This means that information communicated must contain all the required details
        This therefore involves responding to the following questions;-

      • Who is the audience
      • What is the subject matter
      • Why are we communicating the message
      • What time will the information be communicated
      • Where the communication will take place
